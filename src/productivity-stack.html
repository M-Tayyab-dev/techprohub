The 2023 Productivity Stack: Tools That Work Together

Alex Chen

Digital Productivity Consultant & Systems Thinker

Beyond Isolated Apps: The Integrated Approach

The productivity tool market has exploded over the past few years, with specialized apps for every conceivable workflow. Yet paradoxically, having too many disconnected tools can actually decrease productivity. The most effective knowledge workers in 2023 aren't those with the most apps—they're the ones with the most thoughtfully integrated systems.

Core Components of an Effective Stack

An optimal productivity stack addresses five key areas, with tools that communicate seamlessly with each other:

  • Task/Project Management - Capturing, organizing, and tracking work
  • Knowledge Management - Storing and retrieving information
  • Communication - Synchronous and asynchronous team collaboration
  • Calendar/Time Management - Scheduling and protecting focused time
  • Automation - Connecting tools and eliminating repetitive work

2023's Most Powerful Stack Combinations

After analyzing workflows from hundreds of high-performing teams and individuals, three distinct stack archetypes have emerged:

All-in-One Ecosystem

Core tools: Notion, Coda, or ClickUp

These comprehensive workspace platforms aim to be your everything tool. The advantage is tight integration between modules; the downside is that they rarely excel in all areas.

Best for: Small teams that value simplicity and cohesion over specialized functionality.

Best-in-Class Integration

Core tools: Asana/Trello + Obsidian/Roam Research + Slack + Google Calendar + Zapier

This stack combines specialized tools that excel in their respective domains, connected through robust automation and integration services.

Best for: Teams with complex workflows who need depth in specific areas.

The Minimalist Stack

Core tools: Todoist + Obsidian + Google Workspace

This streamlined approach focuses on lightweight, fast tools with careful attention to workflow design.

Best for: Solo professionals and small teams who value speed and simplicity.

Integration is the Secret Sauce

The power of your productivity stack doesn't come from the individual tools but from how they work together. Here are the key integration points to establish:

  1. Connect task creation across all platforms (email → task manager, chat → task manager)
  2. Link calendar with task manager to facilitate time blocking
  3. Ensure bidirectional flow between knowledge base and project management
  4. Create automated connection points between tools using Zapier, Make, or native integrations

Rising Stars of 2023

Several newer tools are transforming how we think about productive work:

  • Command-line productivity tools - Terminal-based apps offering unprecedented speed for power users
  • AI writing assistants - Moving beyond grammar checking to become true collaborative partners
  • No-code automation platforms - Making complex workflow automation accessible to non-developers
  • Digital gardens - Personal knowledge management systems designed for networked thinking

Key Takeaways

  • Focus on integration between tools rather than collecting more apps
  • Choose tools based on your specific workflows, not trending features
  • Invest time in automation to eliminate context switching
  • Regularly audit your stack to remove tools that no longer serve their purpose
